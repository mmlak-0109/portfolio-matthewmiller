Projects

Make Effective Data Visualization

An interactive visualization built using HTML/CSS, JavaScript, dimple.js, and d3.js. The visualization shows how baseball players (grouped by their handedness) compare across their average performance stats, and show how the relationships between players (grouped by their height and weight) compare across their average performance stats.

Design an A/B Test

For this project, I considered an actual experiment that was run by Udacity. I fleshed out the experiment idea into a fully defined design, analyzed the results, and ultimately recommended not to launch the tested website change. I also proposed a high-level follow-up experiment, including a hypothesis, unit of diversion, and which metrics to measure.

Wrangle OpenStreetMap Databases

Wrangled XML OpenStreetMap data for Los Angeles, CA. Used data munging techniques to assess the quality of the data for validity, accuracy, completeness, consistency and uniformity. Used SQL to store, query, and aggregate the data.

Explore and Summarize Data

Used R in Rstudio to explore a data set containing financial contributions made by California residents in the 2016 Presidential election. Discovered that, out of the five parties, Democrats swept the field receiving 85% of donations by count and 76% of donations by dollar amount. Almost all of this came from Hillary Clinton and Bernie Sanders.
